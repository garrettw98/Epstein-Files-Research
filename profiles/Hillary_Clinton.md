# 🇺🇸 Hillary Clinton (Former Secretary of State)

> **Status**: **ASSOCIATE / SUBPOENAED TESTIMONY** (Evidence Level: ◐ DOCUMENTED)

## The Connection

Hillary Clinton appears in the congressional phase of the Epstein-file investigation primarily as a subpoenaed witness, alongside former President Bill Clinton.

## The Evidence in Scope

*   **Congressional subpoenas**: House Oversight subpoenaed both Clintons in 2025.
*   **Deposition record**: Hillary Clinton appeared for a closed-door deposition on February 26, 2026.
*   **Public release**: Video from her testimony was later released by House investigators in early March 2026.

## The 2025-2026 Congressional Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jul 23, 2025 | Oversight subcommittee votes unanimously to authorize subpoenas |
| Aug 5, 2025 | Subpoenas issued to Bill and Hillary Clinton |
| Jan 14, 2026 | Hillary Clinton does not appear for scheduled deposition |
| Jan 21, 2026 | Committee recommends contempt of Congress for both Clintons |
| Feb 3, 2026 | Clintons agree to appear for late-February depositions |
| Feb 26, 2026 | Hillary Clinton gives closed-door deposition |
| Mar 2, 2026 | Reporting indicates House committee releases testimony video |

## Deposition Outcome (Current Public Record)

AP coverage reported Hillary Clinton's testimony included two key claims:

*   She said she had no information on Epstein's crimes.
*   She said she did not recall meeting Epstein.

AP coverage also reported the session lasted over six hours.

## The Defense Position

The Clintons' legal team argued the subpoenas were invalid and politically motivated before eventually agreeing to testimony.

## Verdict

**Subpoenaed witness with completed testimony.** In the tracked record, Hillary Clinton is central to the congressional oversight process but is not currently identified in this repo's primary evidence set as an accused perpetrator.
