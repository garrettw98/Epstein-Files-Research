# Thomas Massie (U.S. Representative, R-KY)

> **Status**: **CONGRESSIONAL ACTOR**
> **Role in Repo**: Transparency Act co-author and unredaction pressure campaign

## Summary

Rep. Thomas Massie is one of the central congressional actors in the 2025-2026 release phase. In this repository, he appears as co-author of the Epstein Files Transparency Act and as a key participant in the February 2026 unredaction confrontation with DOJ leadership.

## Key Timeline

- **2025**: Co-authored the Epstein Files Transparency Act with Rep. Ro Khanna.
- **Feb 9, 2026**: Reviewed unredacted files in DOJ secure access setting with Khanna.
- **Feb 9, 2026**: Publicly challenged DOJ redactions during a live media confrontation.
- **Feb 9, 2026**: Additional names were unredacted from a previously redacted list during this pressure cycle.
- **Feb 11, 2026**: Appeared in the Bondi hearing as a questioner on file handling and redactions.

## Main Claims and Actions

Massie publicly argued that:
- Public release redactions exceeded what Congress expected under the law.
- Certain non-victim names were withheld inappropriately.
- DOJ should unredact additional records or face further congressional disclosure pressure.

He also signaled willingness to use Speech or Debate protections for additional disclosures if DOJ did not expand transparency.

## What Is Documented vs Unresolved

### Documented
- Massie co-authored the statute and participated in secure-room review.
- Massie publicly challenged DOJ over specific redactions.
- Unredaction changes occurred during the same public dispute window.

### Unresolved/Disputed
- Whether all of Massie's redaction criticisms are supported by complete documentary chains.
- Whether DOJ changes were policy-consistent decisions or reactive adjustments.
