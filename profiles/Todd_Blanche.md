# Todd Blanche (U.S. Deputy Attorney General)

> **Status**: **GOVERNMENT OFFICIAL**
> **Role in Repo**: DOJ public response and redaction-process defense

## Summary

Todd Blanche appears in the 2026 release cycle as the DOJ official most associated with public defense of release operations, no-new-prosecution messaging, and real-time responses to congressional redaction challenges.

## Key Timeline

- **Feb 2, 2026**: Blanche publicly stated no additional Epstein-related prosecutions were expected from reviewed materials.
- **Feb 9, 2026**: During the congressional unredaction dispute, Blanche engaged in public exchanges after lawmakers challenged redactions.
- **Feb 9, 2026**: DOJ unredacted additional non-victim names from a previously redacted list during this pressure cycle.

## Public Process Role

In repository records, Blanche is tied to:
- Defending DOJ redaction and release decisions,
- Explaining scale and processing limits for millions of pages,
- Responding to public claims made by lawmakers during the secure-room access period.

## What Is Documented vs Unresolved

### Documented
- Blanche issued no-new-prosecution messaging in early February 2026.
- Blanche publicly commented on specific redaction disputes connected to congressional review events.

### Unresolved/Disputed
- Whether DOJ's redaction standards were consistently applied before and after public pressure.
- Whether public dispute management changed substantive disclosure outcomes.
