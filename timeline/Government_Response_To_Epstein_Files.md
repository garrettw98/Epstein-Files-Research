# Government Response Timeline to the Epstein Files (2019-2026)

This timeline maps how U.S. government response evolved from prosecution activity to transparency and oversight fights.

## Phase 1: Federal Criminal Case Reset (2019-2021)

- **July 2019**: SDNY announces federal sex-trafficking charges against Jeffrey Epstein.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/jeffrey-epstein-charged-manhattan-federal-court-sex-trafficking-minors
- **August 2019**: Epstein dies in federal custody.  
  Source context: https://oig.justice.gov/reports/investigation-and-review-federal-bureau-prisons-custody-care-and-supervision-jeffrey
- **November 2019**: SDNY charges two MCC correctional officers for allegedly falsifying records on Epstein's housing unit.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/correctional-officers-charged-falsifying-records-august-9th-and-10th-metropolitan
- **July 2020**: SDNY announces charges against Ghislaine Maxwell.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/ghislaine-maxwell-charged-manhattan-federal-court-conspiring-jeffrey-epstein-sexually
- **December 2021**: SDNY issues statement on Maxwell jury verdict.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/statement-us-attorney-damian-williams-verdict-us-v-ghislaine-maxwell

## Phase 2: Oversight and Accountability Reviews (2022-2024)

- **2023 (June)**: DOJ Office of Inspector General publishes review of BOP custody, care, and supervision failures in Epstein's detention period.  
  Source: https://oig.justice.gov/reports/investigation-and-review-federal-bureau-prisons-custody-care-and-supervision-jeffrey
- **2024 (January, court unsealing cycle)**: Civil-case document unsealing accelerates public pressure for broader federal disclosure.  
  Repository context file: `evidence/2024_Release.md`

## Phase 3: Congressional Transparency Statute and DOJ Production (2025-2026)

- **February 27, 2025 (DOJ release cycle reference)**: DOJ announces first phase declassified release activity tied to Epstein files.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/attorney-general-pamela-bondi-releases-first-phase-declassified-epstein-files
- **July 15, 2025**: H.R. 4405 (Epstein Files Transparency Act) introduced.  
  Source: https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/119/hr4405
- **November 18, 2025**: Bill passes House (recorded vote 427-1).  
  Source: https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/119/hr4405
- **November 19, 2025**: Bill passes Senate and is signed by the President (Public Law 119-38).  
  Sources:  
  - https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/119/hr4405  
  - https://www.govinfo.gov/link/plaw/119/public/38
- **January 30, 2026**: DOJ announces publication of 3.5 million responsive pages.  
  Source: https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/department-justice-publishes-35-million-responsive-pages-compliance-epstein-files
- **February 9, 2026**: AP reports secure congressional access to unredacted material begins.  
  Source: https://apnews.com/article/5219f89459e80a141b84e1aa2551b0d2
- **February 11, 2026**: House Judiciary oversight hearing with AG Pam Bondi.  
  Sources:  
  - https://judiciary.house.gov/committee-activity/hearings/oversight-us-department-justice-5  
  - https://www.cbsnews.com/live-updates/pam-bondi-hearing-epstein-files-justice-department-congress/
- **February 12, 2026**: AP reports dispute over DOJ logging of congressional search activity in the file portal.  
  Source: https://apnews.com/article/13eeb9ccadcc03ba34922d8ba95ce220
- **February 15, 2026**: Reuters reports DOJ letter to lawmakers saying inclusion of names in records is not itself evidence of criminal activity and describing redaction rationale.  
  Sources:  
  - https://www.yahoo.com/news/us-justice-department-sends-letter-072131716.html  
  - https://keyt.com/news/national-world/cnn-us-politics/2026/02/15/doj-letter-on-epstein-files-lists-hundreds-of-names-but-doesnt-provide-context/
- **February 17, 2026**: Reuters reports New Mexico lawmakers approved a state-level commission process to investigate allegations tied to Epstein's Zorro Ranch.  
  Source: https://www.reuters.com/world/us/new-mexico-approves-comprehensive-probe-epsteins-zorro-ranch-2026-02-17/

## Government Response Pattern (What Changed)

- **2019-2021**: Response was prosecution-centered (indictments, trial outcomes).
- **2022-2024**: Response shifted to institutional review and detention-failure accountability.
- **2025-2026**: Response became disclosure-governance focused (legislation, mass publication, redaction disputes, congressional oversight).
