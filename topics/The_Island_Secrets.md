Little St. James wasn't just a home; it was a fortress designed for privacy and, potentially, escape.

## 1. The "Temple"
*   **The Structure**: A bizarre building painted in blue and white horizontal stripes with a golden dome.
*   **The "Music Room"**: Building permits described it as a "Music Pavilion" with a grand piano.
*   **The Entrance**: Photos show a large wooden door with a locking bar on the *outside* (implying locking someone in?).
*   **The Submarine Theory**: Investigators and architects noted the location (on a cliff edge) would be ideal for a vertical shaft down to a sea-level submarine dock, though a physical elevator shaft has not been publicly verified in the released blueprints.

## 2. The Tunnels
*   **The Myth**: "Underground cities" for holding children.
*   **The Reality**:
    *   **Man-Made**: The island has standard utility tunnels (pipes, cisterns) common in the Caribbean.
    *   **Natural**: The waters around the island (specifically "Cow and Calf" rocks) are famous for **natural coral caves and tunnels**.
    *   **Surveillance**: The "tunnels" myth often conflates the *wiring* tunnels (for the cameras) with human-trafficking tunnels.

## 3. The Submarines
This is verified.
*   **Ghislaine's License**: Ghislaine Maxwell possessed a license to pilot **submersibles**.
*   **Ownership**: Epstein owned a submarine (confirmed in financial records).
*   **The "TerraMar" Cover**: Maxwell's charity, "The TerraMar Project," gave them a legitimate reason to own high-tech underwater equipment.
*   **The Escape Scenario**:
    *   Submarines can leave a dock undetected by radar.
    *   They can travel to international waters ("The High Seas").
    *   If TerraMar had achieved sovereign status (as Maxwell hoped), a submarine in international waters would be diplomatically untouchable.
