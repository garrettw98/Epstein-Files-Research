# The Norway Scandal (February 2026)

> **Status**: **ONGOING** — Multiple Investigations
> **Figures**: Crown Princess Mette-Marit, PM Jagland, FM Brende, Amb. Juul, Terje Rød-Larsen
> **Trigger**: January 30, 2026 DOJ Release

## Summary

The January 2026 Epstein files triggered a political earthquake in Norway, implicating **five of the country's most prominent public figures** — a crown princess, a former prime minister, a former foreign minister, and two of its most famous diplomats. Norway is the single most affected country outside the United States.

## Crown Princess Mette-Marit

### The Evidence
- Name appears **1,000+ times** in the released files
- Exchanged **100+ friendly emails** with Epstein over several years
- **Stayed at his Palm Beach home** for several days in **2013** — five years after his conviction
- Googled Epstein in **2011** and told him the results **"didn't look good"** — yet continued the relationship
- Epstein arranged **cosmetic procedures** for her in Palm Beach
- Received **23andMe genetic testing kits** from Epstein

### Her Response
> *"I must take responsibility for not having investigated Epstein's background more thoroughly. I showed poor judgment and regret having had any contact with Epstein at all."*

### Fallout
- PM Jonas Gahr Støre said she showed **"lack of judgment"**
- Norway's largest sexual health center **"Sex and Society"** cut ties
- Revelations dropped days before her son **Marius Borg Høiby's rape trial**
- Public debate over whether she can remain in the royal family and become queen
- Parliament still voted **overwhelmingly** to maintain the monarchy

## Former PM Thorbjørn Jagland

### The Evidence
- Asked Epstein for **financial help buying an apartment**
- **Stayed with Epstein** in New York (2018) and Paris (2015, 2018) — all post-conviction
- **June 28, 2016** email: *"If Trump wins in US I'll settle on your island."*
- Epstein asked Jagland to arrange a meeting with **Russian FM Sergey Lavrov** so Epstein could provide "insight" about Trump
- Received gifts, travel, and loans during time as **Nobel Committee chair** and **Council of Europe Secretary General**

### Criminal Investigation
- Norway's economic crime authority **Okokrim** opened probe for **"aggravated corruption"**
- Investigating gifts, loans, and paid travel received while holding public office
- Has **immunity** as former Council of Europe head
- Foreign Minister **Espen Barth Eide** announced government will seek to **revoke immunity**

### Response
Jagland's lawyer said his client **"welcomed the investigation"** and was **"calm"** about the outcome.

## Børge Brende (WEF CEO)

### The Evidence
- Former Norwegian Foreign Minister (2013-2017), now **CEO of the World Economic Forum**
- Had **three business dinners** with Epstein (2018-2019)
- Communicated via **email and text message**
- Introduced to Epstein by **Terje Rød-Larsen**
- Epstein sent Brende a photo of **Sara Netanyahu** with an offensive comment; Brende **responded lightheartedly**
- Later said he **"never should have commented on the picture"**

### Investigation
- WEF launched **independent review** via its risk committee
- Under **separate probe** alongside Jagland over Epstein links

## Mona Juul & Terje Rød-Larsen

### Who They Are
A married couple — perhaps Norway's most famous diplomats, known for helping broker the **Oslo Peace Accords** (Israel-Palestine).

### The Evidence
- **Visited Epstein's island** with their children
- In a version of Epstein's will, their **two children stood to inherit $5 million each**
- Rød-Larsen introduced Brende to Epstein
- Maintained extensive contact after Epstein's conviction

### Fallout
- **Mona Juul** (Norway's Ambassador to Jordan and Iraq) was **suspended** pending investigation
- The couple faces intense media scrutiny in Norway

## Impact on Norway

### Institutional Response
- Parliament Foreign Affairs Committee is conducting its own review
- A **majority of parties** in parliament support an independent inquiry into the foreign ministry
- The government is considering new disclosure requirements for public officials

### Public Sentiment
- The scandal is the **largest political crisis** in Norway since the 2011 Utøya attacks
- Media coverage has been described as a **"national reckoning"**
- Despite the scandal, parliament voted to **maintain the monarchy**
