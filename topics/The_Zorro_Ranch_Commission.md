# The Zorro Ranch Commission (New Mexico)

> **Status**: **STATE-LEVEL INVESTIGATIVE PROCESS (APPROVED FEB 2026; SEARCH ACTIVITY REPORTED MAR 2026)**

## Summary

In February 2026, Reuters reported New Mexico lawmakers approved a commission process to investigate allegations tied to Epstein's Zorro Ranch. By March 10, 2026, AP reported investigators searched the former property. In this repository, that sequence marks a shift from federal release disputes and symbolic discussion of the ranch to state-level investigative follow-through.

## Timeline

- **Feb 17, 2026**: Reuters reported commission approval by New Mexico lawmakers.
- **Mar 10, 2026**: AP reported New Mexico investigators searched the former ranch property.
- **Planned output (reported)**: hearings phase and a final report in 2027.

## How the Narrative Changed in March 2026

Before March, Zorro Ranch was easy to discuss only as a location of dramatic allegations: the "breeding" concept, remote-isolation claims, and the unresolved server-room scrub.

The March 10 search matters because it changes the status of the site in the repo:

- It is no longer only a historical symbol in the 2000s abuse architecture.
- It is not only a future commission topic awaiting hearings.
- It became an active place where authorities still believed recoverable evidence or relevant physical context could exist.

That does not prove the most extreme claims made about the ranch. It does show that investigators still view the site as materially relevant after years of federal and media attention.
